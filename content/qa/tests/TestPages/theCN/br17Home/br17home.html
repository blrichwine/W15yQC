                One aspect of our discussion of authentic assessment was whether some majors lend themselves better or worse to it. in majors that stress "fundamentals" in the first few years, it may seem like you can't use authentic assessment for these courses because you have to learn the fundamentals before you can do anything useful with them. However, I think that this is a problem with the structure of the curriculum rather than the nature of the content. Instead of making the student learn fundamentals out of context, we need to create a context that they can be authentically assessed in because research shows that students are more motivated to learn skills when they are being asked to apply them rather than learning them out of context.
